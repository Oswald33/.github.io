Deconstruction Demo Show

Created2020-11-15|Updated2020-11-16
|Post View:

Project Presentation

Demo Video

Introduction

Deconstruction is a single player puzzle game that I developed from July to September this year.

My design inspiration came from another game The Witness. I used simple geometric principles to create a lot of imaginative gameplay. The core gameplay is to use the method of indirect control of AI characters to pass every level. This type of gameplay has the potential to create a variety of puzzles and variants, but not always repeat a routine, and it is also very easy to improve from design perspective.

Level 1

In the first level, players need to indirectly control the character AI by controlling the room block. During the operation, players will notice that the AI character is pathfinding in accordance with fixed rules. In my design, when players do not operate, the character will be in a state of repeating a circular route, so as to facilitate players to observe and avoid being in a hurry. I hope the overall rhythm of the game to be in the experience of observing – trying – discovering, so it is important to make it easy enough for players to observe.

Secondly, I introduced a mechanism: the feature that the character will change its color when players cross the colored edge. And I managed to make players understand the relationship between the game target and this mechanism.

There are only two rooms in this level, but there are various way to complete it. The purpose is to let players practice, familiar with the movement operation and understand the overall atmosphere of the game, which achieves the tutorial that players can understand with no text description.

Level 2

The second level is a complex upgrade of the pathfinding rules of the first level. Players have learned the left and right movement rules of the character’s AI through the previous level, and then the up and down movement rules, namely the rules corresponding to the stairs, are added in this level: the AI will give priority to the stairs going up or down in the same direction, but the stairs going in the opposite direction will not be triggered. In subsequent levels, I will add a few more new rules for AI movement, but not too many for players to memorize.

The second new mechanism is the relationship between doors and buttons. When a character triggers a button, all the doors will open/close at the same time. This setting has brought players possibilities to pass the level, and also joined the game with interference factors. Players need to think about the order of triggering the buttons and the relationship between the doors and the position of AI characters. Now players have to start to make the path planning.

The third new mechanism is the introduction of another AI character. In order to keep players from getting too confused, this level is just an introduction to the second AI character and players do not need to use it to solve puzzles. So at the end of the level, I designed that as long as the final scene is reached, players can always pass through the destination, which enables players to understand the movement characteristics of the second AI character without getting stuck.

Level 3

The third level is a complex upgrade level for manipulating room blocks. After players have learned two pathfinding rules of the AI character through previous levels, I increased the number of rooms and changed the shape of rooms, improving the depth of the path planning puzzle.

Along with the increasing complexity of the triggers, players must begin to master the ability of estimating time to judge when to trigger a button and when to avoid it.

The second AI character has also been used in this level, and players need to make use of it to get the target character to the destination. AI characters can interfere with each other and change their ways of pathfinding. In subsequent levels, players also need to decide which character can get to the destination, which character needs to change color and so on.

Future Improvement

In the later level design, I will add more interesting puzzles around AI characters, rooms and obstacles.

1. Add more AI into the level

It is not just the number of characters that makes the game difficult, but I hope that at the later stage, players will be able to independently determine which AI is used to assist, which is to achieve a certain condition and which is to complete the level. And I will set stage goals, so that players can get satisfaction from each stage. At the same time, different AI will give the puzzle more solutions, avoiding levels to become monotonous and formulaic.

2. Improve room design

In this development, I did not add any physical mechanism, like elasticity. I think if the physical mechanism of the room was more realistic, I could design a lot of imaginative solutions, such as making a character pop up by moving the room block. Secondly, I originally wanted to make the room size changeable, but due to technical problems, it was not realized. If it could be realized, it would greatly increase level’s creativity.

3. The obstacles

Here I did not do the design of character death, in order to decrease the game’s action elements. But the tension of the game is reduced as well. In later design, I can add some mechanisms related to obstacles to give the game more tension.
